How might we

Connect world travelers and help share their stories?

As curious explorers we want to hear about great adventures, with this in mind I crafted an experience to fill that need. The following App concept allows explorers to curate their special experiences while engaging with a community of daring vagabonds.
Inspired by a personal experience
"After years of exploring distant parts of the world I realized I didn't have a platform to capture, share, and document tactical details of uncharted experiences. I noticed this feeling was mutual among others, so I went to the drawing board, here is my creation."

Our Approach

Knowledge gathering and Grouping
1
Discovery

During our discovery we shadowed, distributed questionnaires, interviews, and discussion groups. The union of travel became a beacon in which many people I met would bond over, further validating my hypothesis.

2
Synthesis

Through filtering and grouping key insights surfaced. Through this consolidation I was able to define priority experiences which were later compared against market research.

3
Market Research

Several apps such as 'Trip Adviser', 'Instagram', 'Facebook', and 'Atlas Obscura" enable users to document surface level details. However, none provide the utility to measure the rigor, grit, stamina, and difficulty or adventure travel.

4
Inspiration

We're inspired by travelers unable to recruit friends with similar physical abilities. Many travelers fear relying on others, however this wall is removed once a noval right of passage is overcome.

Interview Insights

We asked and we learned
1
Task Management

"Right now I don't have a way to track or record what I ask my landlord or property manager, it's hard to keep them accountable when there's no paper trail."

2
Discounted Rent

According to the data over 70% of landlords we interviewed would charge less rent if their tenants would maintain minor property upkeep.

3
Building Trust

"There's not really a way to connect my roommates, the landlord, and repairmen. Would be nice if we're all on the same page when something happens. Things often get lost in translation"

Renter and Landlord Challenges

What we learned from talking to them
1
Mobile First

Home related details must be mobile and web accessible to all devices at all times.

2
Your Rights

Renters don't know their rights and therefor can't protect themselves.

3
Communication

Roommates and landlords often struggle to communicate. There's not often one sources of truth when changes or requests are made.

4
Timing

Request are often time sensitive. The status of a request should be displayed at all times to ensure it takes priority.
